# The Great Gatsby

## Overview
Fitzgerald's masterpiece captures the decadence and disillusionment of the Roaring Twenties. Through the tragic story of Jay Gatsby, the novel explores the corruption of the American Dream and the emptiness of wealth without meaning.

## Key Themes
- The American Dream and its corruption
- Class and social stratification
- Love and obsession
- Illusion vs. reality
- Moral decay and materialism

## Notable Symbols
- The green light - Hope and the American Dream
- The eyes of Dr. T.J. Eckleburg - God watching over moral decay
- The Valley of Ashes - Moral and social decay

## Personal Notes
The prose is absolutely beautiful. Fitzgerald's ability to capture both the glamour and the emptiness of the era is unmatched. Gatsby's tragic pursuit of an impossible dream is heartbreaking.
