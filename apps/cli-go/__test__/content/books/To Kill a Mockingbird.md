# To Kill a Mockingbird

## Overview
A powerful exploration of racial injustice, moral courage, and childhood innocence in the Depression-era South. The story is told through the eyes of Scout Finch, providing both innocence and insight.

## Key Themes
- Racial injustice and prejudice
- Moral courage and integrity
- Loss of innocence
- Social inequality
- Compassion and understanding

## Notable Characters
- Scout Finch - The narrator and protagonist
- Atticus Finch - Scout's father, a moral compass
- Boo Radley - The mysterious neighbor
- Tom Robinson - The falsely accused man

## Personal Notes
Atticus Finch's unwavering moral stance in the face of community pressure is inspiring. The novel's exploration of prejudice through a child's eyes makes it particularly powerful.
